Essential Life Skills

About Essential Life Skills

Extracurricular activities for students is a great way to develop essential life skills early on in their careers. Extracurricular helps build student's soft skills by collaborating and socializing with other individuals as well as technical skills for the future. Even within more managerial roles, students will be faced with different situations that will provoke their ability to use critical thinking and their analytical skills. In a sense, this is a great way to develop character for individuals and for students to figure out what they enjoy or where their interest lies.

Although there are several different skills that can be built, below are some examples on how extracurricular activities can build character development and essential life skills which can set up a student for success.

Networking Skills

Networking skills is an essential part that builds character for a student. The ability to share similar interest with other individuals and developing long-term relationships with people is essential. This type of mutual benefit can be beneficial for students in accelerating their professional development for those who are career focused.

Below are some examples of growth that can happen through networking.

  • Public Speaking
Public speaking can improve a lot of different essential skills. One of the most important traits of public speaking is communication. Being able to communicate your message in an effective and coherent manner to an audience takes a lot of practice and confidence. Likewise, on the opposite side of communication, networking also helps with becoming a better listener. Being able to take in information from individuals around you or answering questions from the audience, this will help students take in a wide variety of information that students may disagree with.

  • Self-Esteem
Having self confidence. Being able to put yourself in the spotlight and authentically speaking to a group of people takes a lot of courage. This can help a student who feels nervous when presenting themselves to others in order to build their confidence.

Resume Building

Extracurriculars are a great way for a student to start with their professional development. In our modern society, experience goes a long way in student's careers. For a student who is looking to progress in their professional development, it is never too early to start.

  • Time Management
Depending on the extracurricular, time management will be a big part in life. No matter how much tasks a student may have, there will always be different types of priorities and different stakeholders that will always see their priorities as the most important. It is essential for a student to understand how to deal with conflicting priorities.

  • Collaboration
There will always be a time for a student to work in a group with a common goal. The sense of collaboration is extremely important for students to understand how to work with individuals who may have different perspectives. This ties in very well with developing a mind of trust, and allows the student to learn from each other.

  • Goal Setting
Having goals will always create momentum for motivation as well as direction. This includes creating milestones for every small and big completions as well as the overall goal in hand. Individuals will develop a sense of drive and a sense of personal satisfaction. Prioritize can change which will create setbacks and may help students develop realistic goals for achievements. Joining extracurricular activities can help students build a strong healthy mindset for their futures and help students build soft skills while celebrating the small wins!

Leadership Skills

You can develop your leadership skills through extracurricular activities. Leadership skills goes a long way by how you interact with others. Knowing that you are able to lead a team shows skills that cultivates a mindset of inspiring others, leading others, and can help you learn empathy, which is especially important in leaders.

  • Analytical/Problem Solving skills
Being a leader can immensely improve your analytical skills. This means forming a solution based on the situation. This may be complex or can be quite simple, but either way, critical thinking is important. This ties in with problem solving as well. All leaders are different. While some may solve a problem with logic and reasoning, some might solve situations with more emotions than others. There is no wrong way to solve a problem, but it is especially important to build a foundation of experiences so that when faced with a similar problem, you will be able to tackle it with a broader view.

  • Adaptability
The world is constantly changing. For this, leaders must be agile where environments are likely to be constantly changing. This means being able to adapt to a change of requirements in timelines and accountability may change. For this, this means stepping outside of your comfort zone and being able to work in ambiguity. This is a strong skill to possess in the future.
