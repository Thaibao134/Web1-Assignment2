Community and Belonging

Sense of Community

An essential benefit of extracurriculars is developing a sense of community that is rooted in their shared values and contributions to civic responsibilities. A sense of community means people bear the sense of purpose and belonging, effective social interaction and social opportunity in their communities. Having a sense of community can be a huge benefit for students in order to establish their identity and their own sense of community through extracurriculars in their own way.

Sense of Belonging

There have been many studies that shows having a sense of belonging can enhance social engagement and participation within communities. By being involved and having a sense of community, it can make students feel as though they are a part of something bigger while making a difference. The sense of achievement by completing something big that requires skills and efforts which involves a large portion of individuals can be incredibly rewarding.

Ways To Build Sense Of Community

One of the easiest ways for students to build their own sense of community or to feel that they are a part of a community of belonging is getting involved with different organizations with goals that students align with. A community is built with mutual interdependence, trust, and shared values and goals. With these main elements, students can strive to learn more about themselves while doing work for the community. Personal indentity and the sense of being a part of something big can be a huge intrinsic development for students.

Effective Social Interaction

Students can develop new social skills and opportunities through extracurriculars including self-awareness, engagement, teamwork, and collaboration. Student's tends to be happier as a result of having access to a social network. Good social contact can help communities address challenges by fostering relationships and bonds as well as expanding these networks. Having social opportunities to meet new people and making new friends are a huge benefit for students because humans are a social race. Making friends can help prevent the feelings of isolation and help create a sense of belonging. Quality of life tends to be better with friends since companionship can provide a deeper level of support regarding health challenges such as depression and loneliness. Students will have the ability to meet a whole bunch of different individuals and have the opportunity to make friends in their lives.
